## Goal

Turn your loop into a section with dynamics. Decide which layers enter when, where the energy builds, and where it drops.

## Approach

- Duplicate your clips across 8-16 bars
- Start removing layers from the beginning — build *up* to the full sound
- Think in energy: intro layers are sparse, peak moments have everything
- Use the arrangement view to see the shape visually
- Common structure: 2 bars sparse → 2 bars add element → 4 bars full → 2 bars strip back

## Orchestration Techniques

- **Build-up:** Start with just bass + one element, add a new layer every 2-4 bars
- **Breakdown:** Remove the kick and bass, leave pads and textures, feels like floating
- **Drop:** Everything comes back at once — maximum impact after a breakdown
- **Filter sweep:** Automate a low-pass filter opening over 4-8 bars for gradual builds

## When to Move On

Your section has a clear arc — it starts somewhere, goes somewhere, and resolves. It's not just a static loop anymore. Move to **Extend**.

## Edge Cases

- If the build feels boring, try removing an unexpected element (like the chord) instead of just muting drums
- If you have too many clips to manage, group related tracks mentally: rhythm group, melodic group, texture group
- Don't perfect this section — you'll refine when you extend to the full arrangement
