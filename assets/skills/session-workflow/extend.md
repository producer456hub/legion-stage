## Goal

Build the full arrangement by treating each section of the track as its own mini seed-layer-shape cycle. Verse, chorus, bridge, breakdown — each gets its own treatment.

## Approach

- Map out the structure first: how many sections, what order
- Copy your shaped section as a starting point for others
- For each new section, go back through the earlier stages:
  - **Seed:** What's the core idea for this section? New melody? Stripped-back version?
  - **Layer:** What layers does this section need? Maybe fewer than the first section
  - **Shape:** How does energy flow within this section?
- Connect sections with transitions — 1-2 bar fills, filter sweeps, risers, silence

## Typical Electronic Track Structure

| Section | Bars | Character |
|---------|------|-----------|
| Intro | 8-16 | Sparse, sets the mood |
| Build | 8 | Layers enter, energy rises |
| Drop/Main | 16-32 | Full energy, the core of the track |
| Breakdown | 8-16 | Strip back, contrast |
| Second Drop | 16-32 | Full energy again, maybe with variation |
| Outro | 8-16 | Layers exit, mirror of intro |

Adjust to taste — not every track needs all sections.

## When You're Done

Play the track start to finish. Does it hold your attention? Does each section feel different enough? Are transitions smooth? If yes, you have a track.

## Edge Cases

- If sections sound too similar, try changing the chord progression or introducing a new sound
- If the arrangement feels too long, cut a section — most electronic tracks are 5-7 minutes
- If transitions are jarring, add a 1-bar fill or half-bar of silence between sections
- Save your project before making big structural changes
